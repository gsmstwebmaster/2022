Career & Technical Education

A student's highschool journey

Here at GSMST, our students are required to complete a series of classes and experiences designed as part of our interactive curriculum.

Grade 9
Physeng & Chemistry

In their freshmen year, our GSMST students are required to take a two-hour block class called Physeng (a combination of honors physics and introduction to engineering). While learning about theoretical physics, students are required to implement their physics knowledge in engineering projects that complement the curriculum. Freshmen are also required to take honors chemistry as engineering and STEM require a thorough understanding of both physics and chemistry as well.

Grade 10
Classes & Science Fair

To facilitate students’ interest in the natural sciences, all students are required to take AP Biology in 10th grade. The course is a gateway into AP Chemistry and AP Environmental Science. Additionally, all GSMST students complete the engineering pathway by taking Engineering Applications. In this course, students expand their previous understanding of engineering principles in projects such as CO2 Cars and Wooden Bridges. As an introduction to the history of the globe and building cultural empathy and appreciation, students take AP World History to learn about the various economies, wars, and consequences that led to our modern world today. As STEM students, it is important for us to understand the history of the world as we set out to help the world post-high school and post-college. During this year, students are also required to participate in the Gwinnett Science or Engineering Fair. Due to Covid-19, the past few fairs have been completely virtual but still showcase our students’ amazing and innovative discoveries.

Grade 11
Courses & Internships

As part of the curriculum, all students are required to complete an internship for 4-6 weeks. Students interact with leaders from the professional business world in a field that the student is interested in. Examples range from medical internships to data analysis internships. All students are also required to take a science course of their choice to expand their STEM knowledge. Popular choices include AP Physics and AP Computer Science A

Grade 12
Classes & Year-Round Internship

A continuation from junior year, SCE is a year-round requirement in which students need to secure and complete an internship in a field of their choosing. Furthermore, students are required to take AP Micro and Macroeconomics and AP Language or Literature to develop their understanding of the real-world.

Robotics CLub

Our GSMST Robotics Club is split into two divisions: First Tech Challenge and VEX. Students build a robot from scratch on a team and are exposed to aspects of logistics, logbook, mechanical design, and programming. Our FTC Teams have previously been regionals and state champs while our VEX teams consistently qualify for Worlds.

Technology Student Association

TSA (Technology Student Association) is a club in which students are divided based on their similar passions and interests to create deliverables for project or on-sight events.

Digital Media Club

Digital Media Club (DMC) is vital to GSMST’s daily network of communication. The club handles producing our morning announcements while also creating videos for school events. Students use Adobe Premier Pro and Adobe After Effects to hone their video production skills.

Computer Science Club

GSMST’s Computer Science Club contains multiple divisions such as Cyberpatriots, Game Development, and Girls Who Code. Student officers introduce younger students to Computer Science Principles and applications in the real world. The club also provides tutoring to those who need help with computer science.
